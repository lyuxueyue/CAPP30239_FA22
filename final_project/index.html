What are renewable sources?

In general, renewable sources include solar power, wind, hydro-electric, geothermal and biofuel. In the technical definition,"renewable" is any energy collected from resources that are naturally replenished on a human timescale. In the popular definition "renewable" is equated with "sustainable" and "eco-friendly".

Why pay attention to non-hydro electricity?

Hydroelectric power is an old renewable source. There are barely new hydroelectric dams that could be installed on earth now. Therefore, we add the variable of non-hydro to show the development from other renewable electricity source.

Electricity Generation and Consumption in Selected Countries in the Past 5 Years

Renewable electricity generation in Belgium and Turkey have stepped down in 2021. Nearly 20% of the countries have less renewable electricity generation compared with 2019, and another 20% make no progress in the field.

Zambia, the only developing country that relies on renewable sources for more than 85% of its electricity. The ratio even gets higher after 2020.

We also see a slight increase in renewable electricity in China and Mexico, though relatively smaller than increase in the total generation.

Keeping a steady electricity generation and consumption, New Zealand has a substantially higher proportion of renewable electricity, 87.5%, compared with Argentina and the U.S.
